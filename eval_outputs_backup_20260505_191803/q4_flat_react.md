**Query:** What is the current state of quantum error correction, and what are the main obstacles to achieving fault-tolerant quantum computing?

---

The current state of quantum error correction is an active area of research, with current state-of-the-art quantum computers having error rates that are typically in the range of 1% to 0.1%. However, achieving fault-tolerant quantum computing is still a significant challenge due to various obstacles, including the need for more advanced quantum error correction techniques and the development of more robust quantum systems.
