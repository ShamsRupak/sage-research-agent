**Query:** How does retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) compare to fine-tuning for domain-specific language model applications?

---

Retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) and fine-tuning are two methods used to enhance large language models (LLMs) for domain-specific applications. RAG augments a language model's generation capabilities with external knowledge retrieval, potentially improving its performance and adaptability in new or niche domains. Fine-tuning, on the other hand, involves adjusting the model's weights to fit a specific task or domain. While both methods aim to improve LLMs, they differ in approach, with RAG focusing on incorporating external information and fine-tuning focusing on internal model adjustments. The choice between RAG and fine-tuning may depend on the specific requirements of the domain-specific application, including considerations of performance, efficiency, and adaptability.
