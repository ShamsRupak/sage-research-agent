**Query:** What is the current state of quantum error correction, and what are the main obstacles to achieving fault-tolerant quantum computing?

---

The current state of quantum error correction is actively being researched, with new schemes and architectures being developed to manage errors in quantum computers. The main obstacles to achieving fault-tolerant quantum computing include the development of robust and efficient error correction codes, the improvement of quantum hardware, and the reduction of error rates. Recent studies and research have shown promising results, such as the development of 3D quantum error correction schemes, which could lead to more compact quantum 'hard drives'.
